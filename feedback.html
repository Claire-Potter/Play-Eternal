Recommendations

Angela Jacobs, Children ages 1 and 3

"I love spending time exploring with my children. I recommend a textures game that we regularly play. I make a bowl of jelly, a bowl of rice and a bowl of spaghetti. I will hide a few small toys inside each bowl. The kids love getting in there, exploring the different food textures and finding little treasures."

Jessica Suitcliffe, Child age 8

"My daughter is a sensitive little girl and can really struggle with sensory overload. We have made some sensory boards for her bedroom which help her process and explore. I highly recommend this!"

Anna Reeves, Occupational Therapist

"As an Occupational Therapist I understand the benefit of sensory play. One of the activities I always recommend to parents to do at home is the shaving foam activity. Simply, smear shaving foam on the inside of the shower door. Allow your child to draw pictures, shapes or simply smear the foam around!"

Andrew Edwards, Children 3 and 7

"I have learnt so much since I started using this site. I have also come up with some creative sensory ideas myself. The best thing is, you don't even need to make anything. When we go out to the seaside, we have a nature hunt, looking for shells, rocks, seaweed, anything we can find and compare the feel, the smell, the sounds and how the items are the same or different."
